rogues.matrices.wilk¶

rogues.matrices.wilk(n)¶
wilk Various specific matrices devised/discussed by Wilkinson.

a, b = wilk(n) is the matrix or system of order N. N = 3: upper triangular system Ux=b illustrating inaccurate solution. N = 4: lower triangular system Lx=b, ill-conditioned. N = 5: HILB(6)(1:5,2:6)*1.8144. Symmetric positive definite. N = 21: W21+, tridiagonal. Eigenvalue problem.

References: J.H. Wilkinson, Error analysis of direct methods of matrix inversion,

  1. Assoc. Comput. Mach., 8 (1961), pp. 281-330.
J.H. Wilkinson, Rounding Errors in Algebraic Processes, Notes on Applied
Science No. 32, Her Majesty’s Stationery Office, London, 1963.
J.H. Wilkinson, The Algebraic Eigenvalue Problem, Oxford University
Press, 1965.
