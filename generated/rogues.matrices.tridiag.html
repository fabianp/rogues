rogues.matrices.tridiag¶

rogues.matrices.tridiag(n, x=None, y=None, z=None)¶
tridiag tridiagonal matrix (sparse).

tridiag(x, y, z) is the sparse tridiagonal matrix with subdiagonal x, diagonal y, and superdiagonal z. x and z must be vectors of dimension one less than y. Alternatively tridiag(n, c, d, e), where c, d, and e are all scalars, yields the toeplitz tridiagonal matrix of order n with subdiagonal elements c, diagonal elements d, and superdiagonal elements e. This matrix has eigenvalues (todd 1977)

d + 2*sqrt(c*e)*cos(k*pi/(n+1)), k=1:n.

tridiag(n) is the same as tridiag(n,-1,2,-1), which is a symmetric positive definite m-matrix (the negative of the second difference matrix).

References: J. Todd, Basic Numerical Mathematics, Vol. 2: Numerical Algebra,

Birkhauser, Basel, and Academic Press, New York, 1977, p. 155.
D.E. Rutherford, Some continuant determinants arising in physics and
chemistry—II, Proc. Royal Soc. Edin., 63, A (1952), pp. 232-241.
