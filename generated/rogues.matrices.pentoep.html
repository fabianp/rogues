rogues.matrices.pentoep¶

rogues.matrices.pentoep(n, a=1, b=-10, c=0, d=10, e=1)¶
PENTOEP Pentadiagonal Toeplitz matrix (sparse).

p = pentoep(n, a, b, c, d, e) is the n-by-n pentadiagonal Toeplitz matrix with diagonals composed of the numbers a =: p[3,1], b =: p[2,1], c =: p[1,1], d =: p[1,2], e =: p[1,3]. default: (a,b,c,d,e) = (1,-10,0,10,1) (a matrix of rutishauser).

This matrix has eigenvalues lying approximately on the line segment 2*cos(2*t) + 20*i*sin(t).

Interesting plots are ps(full(pentoep(32,0,1,0,0,1/4))) - `triangle’ ps(full(pentoep(32,0,1/2,0,0,1))) - `propeller’ ps(full(pentoep(32,0,1/2,1,1,1))) - `fish’

References: R.M. Beam and R.F. Warming, The asymptotic spectra of

banded Toeplitz and quasi-Toeplitz matrices, SIAM J. Sci. Comput. 14 (4), 1993, pp. 971-1006.
  1. Rutishauser, On test matrices, Programmation en Mathematiques Numeriques, Editions Centre Nat. Recherche Sci., Paris, 165, 1966, pp. 349-365.
