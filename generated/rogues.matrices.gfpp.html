rogues.matrices.gfpp¶

rogues.matrices.gfpp(t, c=1.0)¶
GFPP Matrix giving maximal growth factor for Gaussian elim. with pivoting

GFPP(T) is a matrix of order N for which Gaussian elimination with partial pivoting yields a growth factor 2^(N-1). T is an arbitrary nonsingular upper triangular matrix of order N-1. GFPP(T, C) sets all the multipliers to C (0 <= C <= 1) and gives growth factor (1+C)^(N-1). GFPP(N, C) (a special case) is the same as GFPP(EYE(N-1), C) and generates the well-known example of Wilkinson.

Reference: N.J. Higham and D.J. Higham, Large growth factors in Gaussian elimination with pivoting, SIAM J. Matrix Analysis and Appl., 10 (1989), pp. 155-164.
